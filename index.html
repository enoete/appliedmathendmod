Question 1 — Braking on an Incline 12 marks
The following diagram shows a 3500 kg automobile which travels down a 10° inclined road at a speed of 20 ms⁻¹. If the driver jams on the brakes, causing his wheels to lock, and the coefficient of friction between the wheels and the road is 0.5, determine:
(i) The deceleration of the automobile after the brakes are applied. 3 marks
(ii) The distance the tires would skid on the road. 3 marks
(iii) The loss in kinetic energy, if the car moves a distance of 5 m after the brakes are applied. 6 marks
Question 2 — Projectile Motion: Golf Ball 12 marks
At a point P, a golfer hits a golf ball of mass 46 g at an angle of 35° to the horizontal. The golf ball travels in the air until it hits the ground at a point Q, which is 20 metres away from P.
(i) Determine the speed of the golf ball at P. [4 marks] 3 marks
(ii) Draw a labelled diagram to determine the position of the ball after 1 second. You must show the ball's vertical distance above the ground and the ball's horizontal distance from point P on the diagram. 3 marks
(iii) Calculate the speed and direction of the ball just before it hits the ground at Q. 6 marks
Question 3 — Collision: Golf Ball and Rock 6 marks
A golf ball collides with a stationary rock at point Q. After the impact, the golf ball rebounds with a speed of 3 ms⁻¹ and the rock moves forward with a speed of 4 ms⁻¹.
(i) Define EACH of the following terms: Impulse and Momentum. 3 marks
(ii) State ONE assumption that must be made about the golf ball and the rock, and hence determine the mass of the rock. 3 marks
Question 4 — Three Coplanar Forces 9 marks
Three coplanar forces of magnitude 500 N, 750 N and 1200 N act at a point in the directions shown in the following diagram. The magnitude of the resultant, R, of these three forces acts at an angle \(\alpha°\) to the horizontal.
(a) Determine the value of R. 6 marks
(b) Determine the location of the resultant, R. Your response MUST include the calculation of the value of \(\alpha\). 3 marks
Question 5 — Two-Particle Collisions 6 marks
Two particles, P and Q, were initially moving towards each other in a straight line. The mass of particle P is 2 kg, the mass of particle Q is \(x\) kg, and the speeds before impact are 5 ms⁻¹ and 6 ms⁻¹ respectively.
(a) Both particles come to rest immediately after colliding with each other. Determine the value of \(x\), the mass of particle Q. 3 marks
(b) If after the collision both particles move with a speed of 3 ms⁻¹ to the left, determine the possible value of \(x\), the mass of particle Q. 3 marks

The diagram below shows the approximate velocity–time graph of a package falling vertically from rest. The package has a parachute attached that is set to open at a point after it falls from the plane. The downward velocity of the package \(t\) seconds after falling out of the plane is represented by \(v\) ms⁻¹.
(i) Describe the motion of the package as shown in the diagram. 3 marks
